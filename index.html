Itinerary

Day 1 : Oct 5 ⇒ Arrival in Antananarivo 🛬

Upon your arrival at Ivato International Airport, you'll be warmly welcomed by our representative who will assist you with your transfer to your accommodation in the city. Take this day to rest and rejuvenate after your journey, preparing yourself for the exciting adventures that await in the coming days.

Day 2 : Oct 6 ⇒ Drive from Antananarivo to Andasibe 🚙

This morning, breakfast and then set off towards Andasibe. Along the journey, you will enjoy various sceneries from highlands to rice fields and small cluttered traditional villages mostly perched on hill sides. The road is steep and winding; with lush vegetation until you reach Andasibe, home of the Indri, the biggest lemur in Madagascar. Upon arrival, visit the Vakona reserve harboring five species of lemurs such as, the black-and-white ruffed lemur, the eastern bamboo lemur, the ring-tails, the browns and the dancing diademed sifaka. You will have fun during your visit here since lots of dance performances, photo-ops and a close encounter with the lemurs will fill your day. And then, back to the car taking you to your hotel.

Pick-up from hotel: 8am

Overnight: Mantadia Lodge ★★★

Day 3 : Oct 7 => Drive back from Andasibe to Antananarivo 🚙

This day, return to the capital, Antananarivo, leaving Andasibe in the morning. Upon arrival in Antananarivo, you will visit the Ambohimanga Rova

Overnight : Radisson Blu Hotel ★★★★★

Day 4 : Oct 8 => Fly from Antananarivo to Morondava ✈️, then drive to Kirindy reserve 🚙

Start your day by catching a domestic flight from the capital Antananarivo (Tana) to Morondava, a coastal town on the western side of Madagascar. Upon arrival, embark on an adventurous 4x4 drive through the rugged terrain, heading towards the renowned Kirindy Reserve.

Once you reach your accommodation in Kirindy, check in and enjoy a delicious dinner, savoring the local flavors of the region.

After nightfall, prepare for an unforgettable experience as you embark on a guided night walk through the Kirindy forest. This nocturnal excursion offers a unique opportunity to witness the reserve's diverse nocturnal wildlife in their natural habitat. Keep your eyes peeled for the elusive fosa (Malagasy civet), the iconic mouse lemurs, and a variety of fascinating insects and reptiles that come alive under the cover of darkness.

Overnight : Relais du Kirindy ★★★

Day 5 : Oct 9 ⇒ Kirindy (2 hours of visit in the forest, then head to Bekopaka crossing the Tsiribihina river)

Wake up early and immerse yourself in the beauty of the Kirindy Reserve with a 3-hour guided forest walk. This dry deciduous forest is home to an incredible array of endemic species, including the iconic fosa (Malagasy civet), various lemur species, and a diverse range of birds and reptiles. Your knowledgeable guide will help you spot and identify the unique flora and fauna as you explore the well-maintained trails.

After this immersive nature experience, it's time to embark on a journey to Bekopaka, crossing the legendary Tsiribihina River. The drive will take you through stunning landscapes, offering glimpses of rural Malagasy life and breathtaking vistas along the way. As you approach the river, prepare for an exciting crossing by traditional ferry, adding to the sense of adventure.

Upon reaching Bekopaka, you'll be greeted by the majestic Tsingy stone forests, a UNESCO World Heritage Site known for its unique geological formations and rich biodiversity.

Overnight : Soleil Des Tsingy Lodge ★★★

Day 6 : Oct 10 ⇒ Bekopaka (Big Tsingy visit - Climbing + Hiking + Suspension bridge)

Today, you'll embark on an extraordinary adventure to the Grand Tsingy, a geological wonder within the Tsingy de Bemaraha National Park. This UNESCO World Heritage Site boasts a unique and surreal landscape of towering limestone pinnacles, carved by natural erosion over millions of years. After a briefing from your expert guide, you'll set off on a thrilling hike through this otherworldly terrain, navigating narrow passages and scaling ladders to explore the intricate network of canyons and caves. Along the way, you'll have the opportunity to spot the park's diverse flora and fauna, including several endemic species found nowhere else on Earth. Towards the end of your exploration, you'll cross the iconic suspension bridge, suspended high above the canyon, offering breathtaking views of the surrounding tsingy formations. The Grand Tsingy promises to be a highlight of your Madagascan adventure, offering an unforgettable experience amidst one of nature's most remarkable creations.

Overnight : Soleil Des Tsingy Lodge ★★★

Day 7 : Oct 11 ⇒ Bekopaka (Small Tsingy visit + Gorge of Manambolo river)

Today you'll discover the Small Tsingy, a maze-like network of sharp limestone needles. Navigate through this intricate geological wonder, marveling at nature's artistry.

Later, venture to the awe-inspiring Gorge of Manambolo River, a deep ravine carved by powerful waters over centuries. Take in the breathtaking views of the gorge's towering walls adorned with lush vegetation and cascading waterfalls. Keep an eye out for the park's unique flora and fauna, including endemic lemurs, birds, and reptiles.

Overnight : Soleil Des Tsingy Lodge ★★★

Depart Bekopaka, embarking on the scenic drive back to Morondava. Evening highlights include witnessing the iconic Baobab Alley at sunset - a mesmerizing sight as the golden rays bathe the ancient baobab trees. Capture unforgettable photographs of these majestic giants silhouetted against the vibrant sky. After this breathtaking spectacle, continue to Morondava for an overnight stay, reflecting on the day's extraordinary experiences.

Upon arrival in the capital, you'll head straight to the west part of Antananarivo, you’ll visiting Lemurs park, There you'll find a variety of Madagascar lemurs, including the Ring tailed lemur (Lemur catta or Maki).

Day 11 : Oct 15 ⇒ Visit Nosy Komba and Nosy Tanikely then head to Nosy Iranja with a private fast boat

The Archipelago Excursion is a popular tourist activity in Nosy Be, Madagascar. It's a full-day boat trip that takes you to the surrounding islands, where you can explore crystal-clear waters, white sand beaches, and unique flora and fauna.

  • Nosy Komba visit
    • Lemur Encounters: Take a guided tour to see the friendly black lemurs that are endemic to the island.
    • Hiking to the Volcano: For the adventurous, hike up the dormant volcano in the center of the island for panoramic views.
  • Nosy Tanikely visit & picnic
    • Marine Reserve Diving: Dive in the marine reserve to see a fantastic array of sea creatures including turtles and colorful fish.
    • Picnicking: Bring a picnic to enjoy on the beach after a morning of exploring the underwater world.
    • Lighthouse Climb: Visit the lighthouse for an impressive view of the surrounding waters and islands.
    • Photography: Capture stunning photos of the wildlife, both underwater and on land, as the islet is a haven for photographers.

Afterwards, heading to Nosy Iranja is a gorgeous idyll, made up of two islets: Nosy Iranja Be and Nosy Iranja Kely. At low tide, they’re connected by a thin, 2km (1.2mi) strip of white sand. Stop for a drink at the hotel on Kely; if you’re lucky, the staff will point out the green turtle breeding spot.

Overnight : SARIMANOK Hotel ★★★

Money, credit cards and expenses

Understanding Money in Madagascar:

  • Currency: The Malagasy currency is the Ariary (MGA), divided into 5 Iraimbilanja.
  • Availability: Ariary is primarily available at local banks, official exchange offices in hotels, and the airport in Antananarivo.
  • Recommendation: We suggest bringing cash in Euros (EUR), British Pounds (GBP), or US Dollars (USD) for exchange. Tip: Exchanging a small amount (200-350 EUR) for Ariary upon arrival at the airport is convenient. You can exchange more throughout your trip as needed. Remember, leftover Ariary cannot be converted back to foreign currency, so exchange smaller amounts at a time. Important: Avoid black markets for currency exchange.

Credit Cards:

  • Limited Use: Credit cards are only accepted in some hotels, primarily in major cities like the capital (Antananarivo) and provincial capitals.
  • Fees Apply: Using your credit card may incur fees depending on your bank.
  • ATMs: Withdrawing cash from ATMs is possible at major banks, but ATMs are not widely available, especially in rural areas.

Bottom Line: Cash is king in Madagascar, especially outside major cities. Plan to use cash for most purchases.

Visa Requirements

For stays in Madagascar, a short-term visa is required. Here's a breakdown of the cost:

  • Less than 15 days: €10 (administrative fee)
  • More than 15 days: €35 (visa fee)

Obtaining Your Visa:

There are two convenient ways to secure your visa:

  1. Apply Online: This is the recommended option to avoid any potential delays upon arrival. Visit the official government website:  https://evisamada-mg.com/en/home
  1. Visa on Arrival: Obtain your visa upon arrival at the airport immigration office by presenting your passport.

Tips and gratuities

We believe tipping is a personal choice for each traveler. However, it's important to understand that tipping is customary in Madagascar, where wages tend to be lower. Consider tipping to show your appreciation for good service.

Here's a general guideline:

  • Drivers: €5 USD per day
  • Local Guides: €3-€5 USD per day
  • National Guides: $8-$10 USD per day
  • Restaurant Waiters: 10% of the bill (adjust based on service quality)

Explanation:

  • Drivers: Drivers typically receive a smaller tip compared to guides.
  • Local Guides: Local guides, who might be specific to a town or region, can be tipped in this range.
  • National Guides: National guides, who are often more experienced and have broader knowledge, can receive a slightly higher tip.
